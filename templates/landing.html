How the Model Works

The system uses a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) model, a type of artificial neural network, to classify URLs as legitimate or malicious. The model has been trained on a large dataset containing 450,000 URL samples. The training process involved extracting various features such as:

  • URL Length: The total length of the URL.
  • Hostname Length: Length of the domain name.
  • Path Length: Length of the path after the domain name.
  • Entropy: Measures the randomness of the URL, indicating its complexity.
  • Suspicious Keywords: Detection of any harmful or misleading keywords in the URL.

These features are then processed through the MLP model, which evaluates the patterns and relationships in the data to predict the likelihood of a phishing attempt. The prediction is displayed as a risk probability, helping users identify potentially harmful websites before they click.
